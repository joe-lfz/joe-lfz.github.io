What is

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder?


OCD, or Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder, is a mental health condition that is characterised by:

  • Recurrent, unwanted thoughts (obsessions)
  • Repetitive behaviors (compulsions)

These obsessions can cause significant anxiety and distress, and may lead to compulsions such as excessive hand-washing or organising items in a specific way. OCD is a chronic condition that can cause significant disruption to a person's life and estimated to affect about 2.3% of the population, and can affect people of all ages.
