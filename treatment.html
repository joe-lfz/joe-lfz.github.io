Treatment Options


It is treated with a combination of medication and cognitive-behavioural therapy (CBT), which can help individuals manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life. Commonly used medications to treat OCD includes: selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) ( a type of antidepressant.) They work by increasing the levels of serotonin in the brain, which can help to improve mood and reduce the severity of OCD symptoms.


Other than medication and CBT, other treatments for OCD may include:

  • exposure and response prevention (ERP) therapy, which involves gradually exposing individuals to their fear or obsession in a controlled environment
  • Teaching them how to resist their compulsions.
  • Therapeutic approaches , such as mindfulness and acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT).
